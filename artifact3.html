Artifact 3: SchoolAI-Assisted Learning Experience – Interactive AI Training Exploration

This artifact captures my **interactive learning session with SchoolAI**, where I explored key **machine learning training methods** through an inquiry-based approach. The experience highlights my ability to leverage AI tools for self-directed learning, critical thinking, and practical understanding of AI/ML concepts.

Project Overview

  • Type: AI-Assisted Interactive Learning
  • Platform: SchoolAI
  • Duration: 2 Weeks (July 2025)
  • Status: Completed

Value Proposition

This artifact demonstrates how **AI-driven learning environments** can foster deeper conceptual understanding. By engaging with SchoolAI using structured questioning, I developed insights into **Supervised Learning**, **Unsupervised Learning**, and **Reinforcement Learning**, while analyzing limitations and ethical challenges of AI systems.

My Learning Approach

I adopted an **Inquiry-Based Learning Strategy**, using progressive questioning to gain conceptual clarity and real-world applications.

  • ✔ Progressive Questioning – starting from basics and diving deeper
  • ✔ Practical Examples – requesting real-world scenarios for each concept
  • ✔ Critical Analysis – questioning limitations and risks in AI deployment
  • ✔ Multilingual Testing – explored AI responses in Hindi and English

Key Learning Outcomes

  1. Supervised Learning: Learned how models train using labeled data; applied this to use cases like email spam detection.
  2. Unsupervised Learning: Understood clustering methods like K-means for pattern discovery without labels.
  3. Reinforcement Learning: Explored agent-environment interactions and applications in robotics and gaming.
  4. Algorithm Importance: Learned role of algorithms in guiding model training and improvement cycles.
  5. Data Quality Impact: Understood how quality data influences model accuracy and bias prevention.

Critical Thinking Demonstrated

During this session, I challenged the AI system by asking questions about its limitations, behavior under edge cases, and ethical concerns:

  • ⚠ "If scammers change patterns, will the supervised model fail?"
  • ⚠ "Why did you skip question 6? Does this reflect an algorithmic limitation?"
  • ⚠ "What safeguards exist to prevent bias in AI models?"

Observed AI System Limitations

  • ❌ Skipped structured questions – indicated gaps in attention consistency
  • ❌ Provided incorrect responses in multilingual context (Hindi → Urdu confusion)
  • ❌ Prioritized task completion over deep engagement
  • ❌ Claimed learning via repetition but lacked session memory

Skills Demonstrated

Skill Description
Critical Thinking Analyzed AI responses for gaps, biases, and limitations
AI/ML Knowledge Deep understanding of supervised, unsupervised, and RL concepts
Problem-Solving Proposed solutions for AI limitations in real-world contexts
Ethical Reasoning Highlighted fairness, accountability, and transparency in AI

Future Applications

This experience inspired me to design a **Fake News Detection System** using supervised learning. I plan to leverage Python (scikit-learn), NLP techniques, and datasets from Kaggle and NewsAPI, incorporating ethical principles like transparency and bias mitigation.
