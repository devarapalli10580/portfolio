Big Data Adoption in Healthcare

Academic Project | Big Data Analytics in Healthcare

Project Overview

This artifact explores the adoption of Big Data technologies in the healthcare sector through a case study on MIMIC-III, a critical care database. The study emphasizes the role of big data in improving patient care, optimizing resources, and driving predictive analytics in clinical settings.

Value Proposition: Demonstrates ability to apply big data principles in healthcare analytics, understand challenges in implementation, and propose strategic solutions for effective adoption.

  • Duration: 3 weeks
  • Role: Data Analyst & Researcher
  • Status: Completed

Project Description

The project focuses on analyzing how healthcare organizations leverage big data frameworks for decision-making. Using MIMIC-III datasets, I examined use cases such as patient readmission prediction, ICU risk scoring, and operational efficiency. The work also covers challenges like data privacy, integration of unstructured clinical data, and compliance with regulations such as HIPAA and GDPR.
