Artifact 3: SchoolAI-Assisted Learning Experience

An interactive AI-driven learning experience where I collaborated with the SchoolAI chatbot to explore machine learning training methods through inquiry and guided dialogue.

← Back to Portfolio

Introduction

This artifact documents my engagement with the Machine Learning Training Methods Coach available on SchoolAI.com, accessed via Professor Metzger's custom link. The chatbot guided me through interactive question-and-answer sessions designed to deepen my understanding of supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

Unlike traditional lessons, this AI-assisted environment allowed me to explore concepts at my own pace, receive immediate feedback, and engage in reflective learning—all while recording interactions for instructor review.

Learning Approach

My approach leveraged inquiry-based learning by posing structured and spontaneous questions to the chatbot. I examined how training methods function, their applications, challenges, and ethical considerations.

  • Engaged in iterative dialogues to build foundational and advanced knowledge.
  • Requested clarifications, examples, and real-world scenarios from the AI coach.
  • Analyzed responses critically to connect theory with practical machine learning workflows.

Chatbot Interaction Highlights

The chatbot provided guidance on key topics including:

  • Supervised learning: Understanding labeled data and model adjustment.
  • Unsupervised learning: Clustering and anomaly detection.
  • Reinforcement learning: Reward systems and exploration vs. exploitation trade-offs.
  • Training algorithms: Gradient descent, learning rates, and optimization techniques.
  • Ethical AI: Bias mitigation, privacy concerns, and transparency.

The AI's prompts helped me reflect on challenges like noisy data, overfitting, and ethical dilemmas, enriching my critical thinking skills.

Key Questions Explored

  1. How does supervised learning utilize labeled datasets effectively?
  2. What approaches characterize unsupervised learning methods?
  3. How does an agent learn optimal behaviors in reinforcement learning?
  4. Why are optimization algorithms critical to model training?
  5. What roles do data quality and quantity play in model accuracy?
  6. How can bias in training data impact AI outcomes?
  7. What ethical practices ensure responsible AI development?

These questions framed my exploration and were enriched through AI-assisted dialogue, which prompted me to consider practical implications beyond textbook definitions.

Value Proposition

This artifact showcases my ability to integrate AI-powered learning tools into my educational journey, demonstrating adaptability, curiosity, and critical analysis skills essential for advanced AI work.

It also reflects my skill to communicate complex AI concepts clearly and thoughtfully, a vital asset for roles that blend technical knowledge with stakeholder engagement.

Reflections

The interactive nature of this chatbot experience allowed me to:

  • Customize learning based on my pace and interests.
  • Strengthen understanding through active questioning and immediate feedback.
  • Recognize the limitations and ethical considerations inherent in AI development.
  • Build confidence in engaging with emerging AI educational technologies.

I intend to continue using AI-driven tools to supplement my learning and professional growth.
