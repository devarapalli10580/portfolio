Artifact 2: AI/ML Timeline Presentation

This artifact presents a comprehensive timeline that traces the evolution of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning from their inception to the present day. It highlights the key milestones, breakthroughs, and paradigm shifts that shaped the AI/ML landscape.

Purpose and Objective

The timeline serves as an educational tool to contextualize the rapid development of AI and ML, making it easier for learners and professionals to understand the historical progression and foundational concepts.

  • To chronicle significant events in AI/ML history
  • To illustrate how AI concepts evolved over decades
  • To provide visual and textual insights for academic and professional audiences
  • To highlight key contributors, technologies, and milestones

Timeline Highlights

1950s: The Dawn of AI

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the famous Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence. The term “Artificial Intelligence” is coined at the Dartmouth Conference in 1956.

1960s-1970s: Early AI Systems

Early rule-based systems and symbolic AI emerge. Programs like ELIZA simulate human conversation. The first neural networks are developed but remain limited due to computing power.

1980s: Expert Systems & AI Winter

Expert systems gain traction in industry, automating decision-making. However, AI suffers from "winters" due to unmet expectations and funding cuts.

1990s: Machine Learning Foundations

Shift from rule-based AI to statistical learning. Algorithms such as support vector machines and decision trees gain prominence. IBM's Deep Blue defeats chess champion Garry Kasparov in 1997.

2000s: Big Data and Deep Learning

The rise of big data and GPUs enables deep neural networks. Breakthroughs in image and speech recognition emerge. AI starts penetrating real-world applications.

2010s-Present: AI Everywhere

AI integrates into daily life — from virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa to autonomous vehicles. Advances in reinforcement learning and generative models expand capabilities.

Design and Development Process

The timeline was created using PowerPoint with enhanced visual elements and interactive components. Key steps included:

  • Researching and verifying historical facts and milestones
  • Organizing information chronologically for clarity
  • Designing visual aids such as icons, graphs, and images
  • Adding detailed notes and context to each timeline point
  • Ensuring accessibility and readability for diverse audiences

Creating this timeline deepened my appreciation for the sustained innovation and occasional setbacks that have shaped AI/ML. It reinforced the importance of historical context in understanding emerging technologies.

The process also enhanced my skills in research, visual design, and presenting complex information clearly and engagingly.
