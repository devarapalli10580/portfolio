Artifact 2: Evolution of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

A timeline-based presentation showcasing the growth of AI & ML from early stages to modern advancements.

Project Objective

The objective of this project was to create an interactive timeline that illustrates key milestones in the evolution of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML). The timeline captures significant events, research breakthroughs, technological developments, and future predictions in AI.

Description & Methodology

This artifact was designed as a visual presentation that explains how AI has evolved over decades. It highlights the major phases: Early AI Concepts (1950s-1970s), AI Winter, Expert Systems Era, Machine Learning Rise, and Deep Learning Revolution.

The methodology included:

  • Researching historical AI developments from academic and industry sources.
  • Structuring milestones into chronological order.
  • Designing a visually appealing timeline using PowerPoint and Canva.
  • Embedding interactive elements and graphical representations.

AI & ML Evolution Timeline

  • 1950: Alan Turing publishes “Computing Machinery and Intelligence” introducing the Turing Test.
  • 1956: Dartmouth Conference – Birth of AI as a field.
  • 1970s: Development of early expert systems.
  • 1980s: AI Winter due to lack of funding and results.
  • 1997: IBM’s Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov.
  • 2006: Emergence of Deep Learning (Hinton’s breakthrough on neural networks).
  • 2010s: Rapid adoption of ML and DL in real-world applications.
  • 2020s: AI for healthcare, autonomous systems, and generative AI models.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • Microsoft PowerPoint – for timeline presentation design
  • Canva – for modern graphics and layout
  • Academic Research Articles – to ensure historical accuracy
