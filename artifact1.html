Artifact 1: COVID-19 Data Analysis – Vaccination vs Death Rates

Analyzing real-world data to understand the impact of vaccination on COVID-19 death rates in India.

Project Objective

The primary objective of this project was to explore the relationship between vaccination coverage and COVID-19 mortality rates in India. By analyzing official data sources, the project aimed to identify trends, correlations, and patterns that demonstrate how vaccination efforts influenced death rates during the pandemic.

Description & Methodology

The analysis involved gathering vaccination and mortality data from government and trusted open data sources. The project was implemented using R programming in RStudio, focusing on data cleaning, transformation, and visualization.

The methodology included:

  • Data Acquisition from official APIs and CSV datasets
  • Data Preprocessing – handling missing values and outliers
  • Correlation Analysis – identifying relationship between vaccination and death rates
  • Visualization using ggplot2 – line charts, bar charts, and heatmaps

Tools & Technologies Used

  • R Programming Language – for statistical analysis and visualization
  • RStudio – IDE for development
  • ggplot2 – for advanced visualizations
  • Data Sources: Government of India COVID-19 dataset, WHO data

Key Insights & Learning Outcomes

  • Increased vaccination coverage strongly correlates with a significant reduction in death rates.
  • Data visualization plays a critical role in understanding health trends and decision-making.
  • Gained expertise in R programming, ggplot2 visualization, and statistical correlation analysis.
